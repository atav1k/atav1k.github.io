Rapid credit for the emerging and mobile

Financing remains either institutional or predatory with few offerings in between to serve an emerging and mobile. What if we could retool credit underwriting to provide a credit service that was quick as it was transparent.

Project Goals

To research, prototype and evaluate credit solutions for emerging and mobile consumers looking to finance their purchases on and offline.

My primary task was to take the company through the design process and produce user tested assets for the product development team to execute on.

Rethinking the lender borrower and experience

The project had begun with certain assumptions about the lending experience drawn from how the industry was positioned. Typically with purchase financing, lenders are the last point in the checkout experience. This proved problematic both to merchants and consumers, who had little trust in an alternate payment method. Integrations are hard for small merchants with few options for brick and mortar business while the rigid credit approval process and a lack of credit availability puts consumers at a disadvantage.

Our generative research showed that placing the lender before the shopping experience solved many of the customer experience pains of credit. This meant a user was approved for a line of credit and had high confidence of receiving credit before shopping as opposed to after leading to a hypothesized increase in checkout conversion. I sketched out a new experience map for rapid credit and drew up possible workflows to capture product screens.

Our evaluative research produced a greater confidence in our product direction and we moved quickly from sketches, to wireframes and intro clickable prototypes that adhered to a our financial technology product principles of convenience, control and clarity. We matched our future thinking product designs with a hypothetical brand identity that emphasized a younger but trustable product before moving on to more evaluative testing and feasibility studies.
